# Institutional-Evolution-and-Technical-Architecture-of-UIDAI
This repository contains a comprehensive evaluative framework and analysis of India’s Digital Public Infrastructure (DPI), specifically focusing on the institutional growth and technical scaling of Aadhaar.

## Project Overview
This study evaluates the Unique Identification Authority of India (UIDAI) as the bedrock of the "India Stack". It analyzes how the system has scaled to support over 1.4 billion people, contributing significantly to India's digital economy.

## Core Research Pillars
### 1. Institutional Evolution

Planning Commission Era: Started as an attached office focused on designing a national identity framework.


Aadhaar Act, 2016: Transformed into an independent statutory body under MeitY, granting strategic and functional autonomy.


Current Scale: Supports over 142.76 crore Aadhaar numbers, facilitating nationwide financial inclusion.

### 2. Technical Architecture & Performance

CIDR Operations: The Central Identities Data Repository (CIDR) processes a daily average of 9.6 crore authentications.
+1


Monthly Volume: Reached a milestone of 231 crore transactions in November 2025 alone.
+1


Advanced Modalities: Integration of AI-driven face authentication alongside traditional fingerprint and iris scans.

### 3. Macroeconomic Impact

GDP Contribution: The digital economy’s share of national income rose from 11.74% (2022-23) to 13.42% (2024-25).


Fiscal Savings: Aadhaar-linked Direct Benefit Transfers (DBT) saved over 4.31 lakh crore by eliminating duplicate beneficiaries between 2015 and 2025.

## System Vulnerabilities & Proposed Reforms
### Technical Strain

Peak-Hour Latency: Simultaneous requests during subsidy disbursements often trigger transaction failures.


Session Management: Frequent E-KYC timeouts cause users to lose session state, leading to digital friction.


Accessibility Barriers: Laborers with worn fingerprints and elderly citizens with fading iris ridges face high "mismatch" errors.
+1

### Strategic Roadmap (Vision 2032)

Microservices Transition: Migrating from monolithic legacy frameworks to independent services (Demographic, Biometric Auth) to prevent system-wide crashes.


Event-Driven Governance: Implementing proactive alerts (e.g., via WhatsApp) for mandatory updates when children turn five.


Future-Proofing: Integration with ONDC to reduce merchant verification costs and adopting quantum-resistant encryption.
+1
